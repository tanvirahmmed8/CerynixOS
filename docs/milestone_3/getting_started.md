# Getting Started with CerynixOS

Welcome to CerynixOS! You are installing the world's first local-first AI Operating System. Because the AI is baked directly into the OS, none of your data ever leaves your computer.

## 1. Installation
1. **Download the ISO:** Get the latest `cerynixos-X.X.X.iso` (approx. 2.5GB) from our official website.
2. **Flash to USB:** Use a tool like [BalenaEtcher](https://etcher.balena.io/) or [Rufus](https://rufus.ie/) to write the ISO to a USB flash drive (at least 8GB).
3. **Boot:** Restart your computer and press F12 (or your PC's boot menu key) to boot from the USB.
4. **Encrypt:** During installation, you will be prompted to create a Full Disk Encryption (LUKS) password. **Do not forget this password.** Without it, your data cannot be recovered.

## 2. Using CerynixAI
Once booted into your new GNOME desktop, you have a fully functional, offline AI assistant at your fingertips.
- Look at the top-right corner of your screen (the system tray).
- Click the **Sparkle Icon (✨)**.
- A beautiful glassmorphism window will slide out. This is CerynixAI.
- You can ask it to write code, summarize files, or tweak system settings. Because of our "Zero-Trust" Action Broker, the AI will always ask for your permission before executing a system command.

## 3. Automatic Updates
You never have to worry about manually patching your system.
CerynixOS checks for updates securely every 24 hours in the background. When an update is available, you will receive a standard desktop notification. Simply click it to apply the update and reboot.
